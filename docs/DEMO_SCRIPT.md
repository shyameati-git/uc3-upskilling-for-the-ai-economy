**Lori, calm and factual:**

> "85 percent of autistic adults in America are unemployed or underemployed."

**Point to the stat.**

> "Not because they can't do the work. Many are exceptional at it — pattern recognition, deep focus, systematic thinking. The barrier is environment."

> "They spend three times more energy masking, navigating ambiguity, and performing neurotypicality than on actual work. The gap isn't skill. It's support."

**Advance.**

---

## SLIDE 3: Our Solution (40 seconds)

**Vikas takes over, standing beside the screen:**

> "BuddyWork is an AI job coach that lives on your phone. Three capabilities."

**Point to each pillar:**

> "See — an AR camera overlay that reads the workspace and highlights what needs attention."

> "Hear — a voice coach that reads every instruction aloud, hands free, at whatever pace you need."

> "Do — one clear action per screen. You never have to guess what comes next."

**Advance.**

## SLIDE 4: Autism-Informed Design (30 seconds)

**Aysu, brief and visual:**

> "This isn't accessibility bolted onto a regular app. Every design choice came from how autistic brains actually process information."

**Gesture broadly at the grid:**

> "One thing at a time. Shapes plus color, never color alone. Voice you can slow down or speed up. Sensory controls you can reach in one tap. And three support levels — the same app adapts from independent to fully guided."

**Advance.**

## SLIDE 5: Live Demo (2 minutes)

**Demo person (Mukti or Shyameati) holds up phone or shares screen.**

**Vikas narrates while demo person taps:**

> "Here's what BuddyWork looks like in action. Our worker opens the app."

**Demo person taps Shelf Scan.**

> "Today's first task: Shelf Scan in Aisle 5. The app gives a preview of the three steps, then says Go."

**Tap Start → tap I'm Here.**

> "Voice coach says: walk to Aisle 5, look for the number above the shelves. Worker taps I'm Here when they arrive."

**Tap Open Camera.**

> "Now point the camera at shelf two. The app shows which shelf to pick — no counting confusion."

**Tap Scan Now. Wait for scanning animation.**

> "The scan runs. AR overlay reads the spine labels. Watch the progress."

**Scanning completes. Results appear.**

> "Four books correct — green circles. Two books wrong — yellow triangles. The app knows which ones and where they should go."

**Tap Fix Them. Walk through fix steps.**

> "Now it guides the fix. One book at a time. Take out FIC HER. Take out FIC DIC. Put DIC in first — D comes before H. Put HER after it."

**Tap through each Done.**

> "Each step: one instruction, one button. No ambiguity."

**Celebration screen appears.**

> "Done. The shelf is correct. Stars earned, streak updated, option to take a break."

**Pause.**

> "That entire interaction — the worker's eyes were mostly on the shelf, not the screen. The app coached, they executed. That's the division of labor."

**Advance.**

## BACKUP Q&A ANSWERS

**"How does it work without internet?"**
> Task sequences and voice scripts can be cached locally. The AR scanning needs connectivity, but the step-by-step coaching works offline.

**"How do you handle workers who can't read at all?"**
> Level 3 mode uses picture-based instructions and continuous voice — reading is never required. Every instruction has an icon equivalent.

**"What about privacy?"**
> Camera processing can run on-device. Worker profiles are stored securely with the employer. No personal data leaves the system.

**"How do employers create new tasks?"**
> Task builder: describe the task in plain language, AI generates the step-by-step breakdown. Job coach reviews and adjusts. Load once, use forever.

**"How is this different from a regular checklist app?"**
> Checklists don't adapt to support level, don't coach through ambiguity, don't use AR to show spatial information, and don't manage sensory load. BuddyWork does all four.

**"What's your business model?"**
> Employer subscription per worker. Replaces or augments human job coaching at a fraction of the cost. Job coaches become system designers instead of repeating instructions.
